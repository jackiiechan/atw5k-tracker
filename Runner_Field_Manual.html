Chapter I
📜Your Quest Begins
Read this before anything else.
Mission Briefing

You have been selected to join an elite team of young runners on a globe-spanning adventure. Over 11 weeks, you will train twice a week — Tuesday and Thursday — visiting legendary destinations along the way. Each mile you run earns Relics, the in-game currency of this world. Spend Relics at The Shop. Earn enough and you unlock gear, powers, and titles. The final destination: the 5K Race Finish Line on June 6, 2026.

⚙ How a Session Works

  • Show up. Attendance is the first gate. No show = no Relics.
  • Buy your Passport Stamp (5 Relics). This unlocks the shop for that day. If you don't have 5 Relics yet — run your way there first.
  • Run your assigned group workout. Miles are logged by your Guide.
  • Relics are awarded — 15 per mile, automatically calculated and added to your balance.
  • Spend your Relics in The Shop before you leave. Unspent Relics carry over to the next session.
  • Watch the Leaderboard. Rankings update live. Your position reflects total miles traveled, not just Relics.
⚠ Important Rule

The Passport Stamp costs 5 Relics and is required every session. You cannot shop, unlock items, or use any reward without stamping in first. Think of it like punching your ticket before boarding the train.

🏆 How to Win

There is no single winner. Every runner who crosses the 5K finish line on June 6 completes the quest. But runners who earn more Relics, collect more items, and climb the leaderboard go down in history as Legends. Your final title, avatar, and background are your trophies.

· · · ✦ · · ·
Traveler's Tip

Every Relic you earn is permanent — even after spending. The leaderboard tracks total miles run, so the only way to climb is to run. No shortcut. No trade. Just distance.

Saving Strategy

Don't blow all your Relics early. The Runner's Bracelet (30 ⬡) is the single most important purchase — save for it fast. After that, decide: cosmetics (avatar, background, title) or experiences (lead warm-up, pick the song). Both are valid builds.

Extra Relics for runners who go above and beyond.

Beyond the miles you log, your Guide can award Bonus Relics for standout behavior during a session. These aren't guaranteed — they're earned by being exceptional. Four bonus types exist:

Guide Note

Bonuses are Guide-awarded and applied in the tracker. Runners do not award their own bonuses. If you think you earned one, ask your Guide — they'll decide.

The 5K

What Is a 5K?

3.1 miles. That is it. Eleven weeks of training with your team is specifically designed to get you ready to run 3.1 miles without stopping. By Race Day, you will have run this distance many times in training — Race Day is just the victory lap.

🎯 Race Day Goals

  • Start with your group. Don't sprint out at the beginning. Your training pace is your race pace.
  • Don't stop. Walk breaks are allowed — but every runner who trained with us can finish without them.
  • Find someone to pace with. Eleven weeks of team training means you know your pack. Run with them.
  • The finish line is the only finish that matters. Your time is yours. Cross it.
· · · ✦ · · ·

📋 Week 11 — Race Week Protocol

The final week is not about gaining new fitness — it's about showing up sharp. Sessions will be shorter. Focus on staying loose, staying hydrated, and getting rest. Race week rules:

Tuesday Session

Short shakeout run. Light, easy pace. Review your race-day plan with your Guide.

Thursday / Race Eve

Rest. Sleep early. Eat a normal dinner — not too much, not too little. Lay out your gear the night before.

Saturday — Race Day 🏁

Arrive early. Warm up with your team. Run your race. Collect your Relics for the finish line mile. Celebrate.

You've Already Done The Hard Part

If you showed up, stamped in, and ran your sessions — Race Day is a celebration, not a challenge. The training is the hard part. Race Day is the reward.
